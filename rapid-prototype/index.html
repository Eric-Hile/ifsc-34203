A word that has two or more different meanings is called a homonym. Homonym A homonym is a word that shares the same spelling or pronunciation as another word but has a different meaning. Examples: Bat A flying mammal A piece of sports equipment used in baseball Bank A financial institution The side of a river Light Illumination Not heavy Related Terms (often confused) Term Meaning Example Homonym Same spelling or sound, different meaning bat Homograph Same spelling, different meaning (may sound different) lead (to guide) / lead (metal) Homophone Same sound, different spelling and meaning their / there / they’re Another useful term In linguistics, a word with multiple meanings can also be called polysemy. Example: “run” run a race run a business run a program (in computing) Since you’re studying computer science, many technical words are polysemous: port (network port / physical connector) host (server / event host) thread (CPU thread / forum thread) If you'd like, I can also show you some of the most confusing English words with multiple meanings — some of them have 10–50 meanings and are surprisingly common. what else might you call these Words that have multiple meanings can be referred to by several different terms depending on the context: 1. Polyseme / Polysemous word From the linguistic concept polysemy. Means one word with multiple related meanings. Example: “Head” Head of a person Head of a company Head of a table These meanings are related through the idea of “top” or “leader.” 2. Homonym Words that are spelled or pronounced the same but have different meanings. Example: “Bat” Animal Baseball bat 3. Homograph Words that are spelled the same but may have different pronunciations and meanings. Example: “Lead” /leed/ – to guide /led/ – the metal 4. Homophone Words that sound the same but are spelled differently and have different meanings. Example: their / there / they’re 5. Double entendre A phrase or word with two meanings, often humorous or suggestive.
